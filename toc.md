## 7. Referral Program

### 7.1 Eligibility and Enrollment
Revel offers a referral program that allows eligible users ("Referrers") to earn a share of platform fees generated by organizations they refer to the Platform. Participation is by invitation only — Revel assigns referral codes at its discretion. Each user may hold at most one referral code.

### 7.2 How It Works
When a new user registers on Revel using a Referrer's code and subsequently creates an organization that generates paid ticket sales through the Platform, the Referrer earns **15% of the net platform fees** (excluding VAT) collected from that organization's ticket sales. This share applies to all paid ticket transactions processed through Revel/Stripe for the lifetime of the referral relationship, unless modified or terminated in accordance with these Terms.

For clarity:
- The revenue share is calculated on **Revel's net platform fees only**, not on ticket sale revenue or Stripe processing fees.
- The referral attribution is **permanent** — there is no expiration window once a referral is established.
- Deactivating a referral code prevents new sign-ups but does not affect earnings from existing referrals.

### 7.3 Referral Attribution
Referral attribution uses a **last-touch model**: if a user encounters multiple referral codes before registering, the most recent code (stored in a browser cookie for 30 days) determines which Referrer receives credit. Each new referral link click overwrites any previously stored code.

If a user **manually enters a referral code** during registration, that explicit entry always takes precedence over any cookie-stored code, regardless of timing.

Once a referral is established at registration, it is permanent and cannot be reassigned.

### 7.4 Independence Requirement
Referrers must be independent from the organizations they refer. Specifically, a Referrer **may not** be:
- An owner, representative, or authorized agent of the referred organization
- An employee, contractor, or consultant of the referred organization
- A person with decision-making authority within the referred organization

Passive membership in a referred organization (e.g., as a regular member or attendee without administrative or decision-making power) is permitted. If a Referrer's relationship with a referred organization changes such that they gain decision-making authority, the referral for that organization is immediately revoked and no further earnings will accrue from it.

### 7.5 Payouts
- Payouts are calculated monthly based on the prior month's platform fee revenue.
- **Minimum payout threshold:** Payouts below **€5.00** are carried forward to the next period until the accumulated amount meets the threshold.
- Payouts are disbursed in **EUR** via Stripe Connect. Referrers must complete Stripe Connect onboarding to receive payouts. Unclaimed payouts (where the Referrer has not completed Stripe onboarding) are not held indefinitely and may be forfeited after 12 months of inactivity.
- No minimum earnings are guaranteed. If referred organizations cease operations, stop selling tickets, or generate no platform fees, the Referrer earns nothing for that period.

### 7.6 Self-Billing Agreement
Before payouts can be processed, Referrers must agree to **self-billing** (Gutschrift). By agreeing, the Referrer consents to Revel issuing invoices on the Referrer's behalf for each payout. Referrers who are registered businesses with a validated EU VAT ID will receive self-billing invoices (Gutschriften) with applicable VAT treatment. Individual Referrers without a VAT ID will receive payout statements.

### 7.7 Tax Responsibility
Referrers are solely responsible for declaring and paying any taxes owed on referral income in their jurisdiction. Revel does not withhold taxes, and the referral relationship does not constitute employment, agency, or partnership.

### 7.8 Prohibited Conduct
Referrers may not:
- Create fake organizations or user accounts to inflate referral earnings
